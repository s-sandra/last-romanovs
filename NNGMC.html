NaNoGenMoCat

About the Project

The National Novel Generation Month Catalog is a comprehensive online database of entry metadata and aggregate statistics for NNGM entries, founded by UMW Digital Studies professor Zach Whalen, to increase the accessibility of NNGM works for creative coding and academic research. Encompassing over five hundred novels across the event’s six year history, it includes such information as source material, vocabulary size and utilized techniques, as well as describes each entry’s content, inspiration and context. As part of an undergraduate research project conducted together with Alex Rudenshiold and Cristina Montemorano, I edited and contributed to this database by documenting over 200 novels. We will present our findings on the larger themes and trends within NNGM entry genres and text generation methods at the Virginia Humanities Conference on March 28.

NaNoGenMoCat can be accessed through this website's catalog navigation tab or by visiting nngm.botstudies.org. Note, however, that NNGMC is a work-in-progress. Entry descriptions have not yet been added and some features, such as timeline and search by author, may not be functional.

Documentation Process

My work for NaNoGenMoCat consisted of writing descriptions for the database, as well as reviewing and correcting errors in existing entry metadata. This entailed reading each entry’s issue comments, repository readmes, code and novel output on GitHub. Descriptions consisted of the novel’s name and output explanation, as well as any inspiration or context explained by the programmer. In addition to descriptions, I also identified the genre and text generation method of each entry, as well as any existing source text used to create the novel.

During the project, I cataloged and reviewed over 215 completed entries from 2017 and 2018, as well as part of 2016 and 2019. This involved collecting additional metadata about the entry’s issue URL, programming language, author, completion date and license. I also identified broken or incorrect URL links, missing programming languages and licenses and inaccurate completion dates among 2017 and 2018 entries.

Database Overview

As of February 2020, NaNoGenMoCat encompasses 532 entries completed between 2013 and 2019. Out of a total of 45 different languages and platforms, 300 novels were created using Python, followed by 101 JavaScript projects and 31 Ruby programs. Entrants employ a diverse range of text generation methods and outputs, which include the following.
